Chapter: 1 - Algebra

METHOD: Solving a linear equation in one variable

Step 1: Collect all terms containing the variable on one side of the equation and constants on the other.
Step 2: Add or subtract the same quantity from both sides to isolate the variable term.
Step 3: Multiply or divide both sides by the coefficient of the variable to get the solution.
Step 4: Substitute the solution back into the original equation to verify.

Example:
Solve 2x + 3 = 7.
Step 1: 2x = 7 - 3 = 4
Step 2: (already isolated)
Step 3: x = 4/2 = 2
Step 4: 2(2) + 3 = 7. Correct.

--- Additional Topics ---

Topic: Quadratic equations
Method:
1. Move all terms to one side to get ax^2 + bx + c = 0.
2. Use factorization, completing the square, or the quadratic formula x = (-b ± sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)) / (2a).
3. Verify roots by substitution.

Topic: Factoring polynomials
Method:
1. Look for common factors.
2. Use grouping for four-term polynomials.
3. Apply formulas (a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b), perfect square trinomials).

Topic: Solving systems of linear equations
Method:
1. Use substitution or elimination to reduce to one equation in one variable.
2. Alternatively, use matrix methods (Gaussian elimination).

Topic: Basic differentiation (calculus)
Method:
1. Use power rule: d/dx x^n = n x^(n-1).
2. Apply product, quotient, and chain rules as needed.

Topic: Basic integration
Method:
1. Use power rule for integrals: ∫ x^n dx = x^(n+1)/(n+1) + C (n ≠ -1).
2. Use substitution and integration by parts when applicable.

Topic: Trigonometry identities
Method:
1. Memorize key identities: sin^2 x + cos^2 x = 1, angle addition formulas.
2. Use identities to simplify expressions and solve equations.

Topic: Geometry — area formulas
Method:
1. Triangle: A = 1/2 * base * height.
2. Circle: A = π r^2.
3. Rectangle: A = length * width.

Topic: Probability basics
Method:
1. P(A) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes.
2. For independent events, P(A and B) = P(A)P(B).

Topic: Sequences and series
Method:
1. Identify arithmetic (difference constant) vs geometric (ratio constant) sequences.
2. Arithmetic: nth term a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d, sum S_n = n/2 (2a_1 + (n-1)d).
3. Geometric: nth term a_n = a_1 r^(n-1), sum S_n = a_1(1-r^n)/(1-r). Infinite geometric converges if |r|<1.

Topic: Matrices
Method:
1. Operations: addition, scalar multiplication, matrix multiplication.
2. Determinant and inverse for square matrices; use inverse or row reduction to solve linear systems.
3. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors (basic idea and computation for small matrices).

Topic: Complex numbers
Method:
1. Write z = a + bi; conjugate z̄ = a - bi; modulus |z| = sqrt(a^2 + b^2).
2. Use polar form r(cos θ + i sin θ) and De Moivre's theorem for powers and roots.
3. Perform arithmetic and solve equations with complex roots.

Topic: Logarithms and exponents
Method:
1. Laws: a^m a^n = a^(m+n); log(ab)=log a + log b, etc.
2. Solve exponential and logarithmic equations by rewriting forms.
3. Use change of base: log_b a = log_k a / log_k b.

Topic: Limits (calculus)
Method:
1. Evaluate limits by substitution when possible; factor or rationalize for indeterminate forms.
2. Use L'Hôpital's rule for 0/0 or ∞/∞ cases.
3. Understand limits as foundation for continuity and derivatives.

Topic: Statistics — descriptive
Method:
1. Mean, median, and mode definitions and calculation.
2. Variance and standard deviation formulas and interpretation.
3. Understand skewness and basic distribution shapes.

Topic: Combinatorics — counting
Method:
1. Permutations: n! / (n-k)! for ordered selections.
2. Combinations: n choose k = n! / (k!(n-k)!) for unordered selections.
3. Use inclusion-exclusion for overlapping counts.

Topic: Number theory basics
Method:
1. Compute GCD using Euclidean algorithm; LCM via product/GCD relation.
2. Prime factorization and its uses.
3. Work with modular arithmetic and congruences.

Topic: Vectors
Method:
1. Represent vectors, add, subtract, and scale.
2. Dot product (gives projection), cross product (3D, gives area and perpendicular vector).
3. Apply vectors to geometry and physics problems.

Topic: Proof by induction
Method:
1. Prove base case (usually n=1 or 0).
2. Assume statement for n (inductive hypothesis).
3. Prove for n+1 using the hypothesis.

Topic: Inequalities
Method:
1. Solve by isolating terms; reverse inequality when multiplying/dividing by negative numbers.
2. Use common inequalities: AM-GM, Cauchy-Schwarz for bounds.
3. Solve linear and quadratic inequalities using sign analysis or intervals.

Topic: Coordinate geometry
Method:
1. Line: slope-intercept y = mx + b; slope m = (y2-y1)/(x2-x1).
2. Distance formula: sqrt((x2-x1)^2 + (y2-y1)^2); midpoint formula.
3. Equations of circles and conic sections (parabola, ellipse, hyperbola basics).

Topic: Transformations (geometry)
Method:
1. Definitions: translation, rotation, reflection, dilation.
2. Represent linear transformations with matrices.
3. Compose transformations and understand effects on shapes.

Topic: Set theory — basics
Method:
1. Definitions: union, intersection, complement, subset.
2. Use Venn diagrams to visualize relationships.
3. Apply set identities and De Morgan's laws.

Topic: Binomial theorem
Method:
1. Expand (a + b)^n = sum_{k=0}^n C(n,k) a^{n-k} b^k, where C(n,k) = n choose k.
2. Use Pascal's triangle to find coefficients.
3. Apply to problems involving coefficients and expansions.
